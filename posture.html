Wearable Posture Corrector Device Overview


The posture corrector device is designed to be low powered that can accurately detect slouching. The SEFR machine learning algorithm was selected because it is fast during prediction and requires minimal memory. The device notifies the user when they are slouching by alarming a buzzer. The buzzer alarms when the device detects slouching for 7 consecutive seconds. In that way, it reduces false positive alarms as it is normal to ocassionally slouch when doing daily activities. The device utilizes a power switch and charging module so that the device can be recharged and switched on and off with leisure. The battery lifespan is optimized to 4 and 1/2 days under ideal conditions by decreasing the system clock frequency of the Arduino Pro Mini and by removing unused internal LEDs from the components used. Instead of using a more noiseless approach like a vibration motor, I believe a buzzer sound is more annoying and it gives more of an incentive to stop slouching. Nevertheless, the piezo can be replaced with a vibration motor if it were to be worn in a professional setting where you don't want others to hear the device.
